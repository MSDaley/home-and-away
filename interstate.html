Interstate Park

Interstate Park is located about an hour and 10 minutes drive

north and east of the twin cities. It got its name from the

fact that half of the park is in Minnesota and half in

Wisconsin, straddling the St Croix River. It's most noted for

having several geological points of interest,

such as the "potholes" that formed when the sandstone eroded

away and left the surrounding granite. We will go down into

one of those. Although hiking on your own is good for the soul,

it's usually better to bring a friend with to share the experience.
